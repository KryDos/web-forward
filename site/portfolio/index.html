WebFWD Portfolio: Fellows

The projects below are the heart of WebFWD. Behind each project is an awesome team working toward the execution of an amazing idea that can move the Web forward. We created the WebFWD program to support the work of Fellows and Friends around the world.

BigBlueButton

BigBlueButton Inc. is a not-for-profit organization dedicated to providing remote students a high-quality learning experience through BigBlueButton, an open source web conferencing system for distance education. Using BigBlueButton, remote students can interact with teachers through real-time sharing of audio, video, chat, presentations, and desktops. BigBlueButton records sessions for later viewing and uses popcorn.js for playback in HTML5.

Website: bigbluebutton.org
Team: Fred Dixon, Richard Alam, Marco Calderon, Felipe Cecagno, Denis Zgonjanin, Jeremy Thomerson, Tiago Jacobs
Twitter: @bigbluebutton

CASH Music

CASH Music is a nonprofit organization focussed on artist sustainability through education and open technology. We’ve worked directly with artists and independent labels to build a free and open platform that can be downloaded and used to promote, distribute, and sell music.

Website: cashmusic.org
Team: Jesse von Doom, Maggie Vail, Jonathan “Duke” Leto
Twitter: @cashmusic

Entropy Wave

Entropy Wave is a San Francisco based company specializing in open video technology. We provide complete systems for video encoding and distribution to content producers and distributors, as well as an open source, Gstreamer-based software media platform for application and embedded developers, Entropy Wave products enable customers benefit from the freedom and cost-effectiveness of open source and open video technology.

Website: entropywave.com
Team: Tristan Crane, David Schleef

Open Photo

OpenPhoto is an Open Source photo sharing platform which gives users complete ownership and portability of their photos. Users’ photos, tags and comments are seamlessly stored into personally owned cloud services owned by themselves. Whatever they want to do or wherever they want to go their photos are free to go with them.

Website: theopenphotoproject.org
Team: Jaisen Mathai, Patrick Santana, Patrick Boos, Florian Menevis, Preet Jassi, Klaus Komenda, Kitt Hodsden, Peter Sankauskas, John Fabrizio, Gareth Greenaway
Twitter: @openphoto

Meemoo

Meemoo is a web application that encourages remixing and experimentation with web media. A Meemoo composition is a graph of modules and the wires that connect them. Modules are HTML pages that can live anywhere online, using any web technology. Wires represent how the modules send messages to each other. Compositions will be sharable on a community site. All compositions and modules will be forkable in the open source spirit of creative collaboration.

Website: meemoo.org
Team: Forrest Oliphant
Twitter: @forresto

Synbiota

Synbiota is building a bio-lab in a browser. Synthetic Biology promises to revolutionize the world in ways we have yet to imagine. Synbiota is working towards this revolution by providing powerful, accessible and open tools for synthetic biology design to the world.

Website: synbiota.com
Team: Connor Dickie, Mason Edwards, Justin Pahara
Twitter: @synbiota

Tomahawk

Tomahawk gives you to access to all of your favorite music in one powerful media player. Seamlessly jump from playing a song off your local machine, to one streaming from your other machines, to a recommendation from Spotify, to a pre-release on SoundCloud to your favorite indie band on Jamendo. Tomahawk lets you plug-in multiple content sources, connect to your friends across a range of networks and breaks down the barriers between true social music experiences by enabling data portability and sharing across music services and licensing territories.

Website: tomahawk-player.org
Team: Leonardo Franchi, Jason Herskowitz, Jeff Mitchell
Twitter: @tomahawk
