Mozilla WebFWD Team

WebFWD is led by a team of professionals with deep experience in startups and entrepreneurship. We're keen to learn as much from our teams as they will learn from us and the program.

Pascal Finette

Pascal Finette is Director of Mozilla's Open Innovation Group, where he works with the wider community inventing the future of the Web. He loves technology and believes that the Internet is deeply impacting mankind. Prior to Mozilla, he led eBay's Platform Solutions Group, consulted entrepreneurs on their strategy & operations and invested into startups, among other things. Craig Newmark (Craigslist) described Pascal as "a social change maker to watch."

Pascal frequently speaks about Open Innovation on the Web and mentors entrepreneurs at TechStars, Seedcamp, The Unreasonable Institute and many other places.

When he doesn't work you can find him running, teaching pro-runners Pilates or preparing espresso shots.

Diane Bisgeier

Diane Bisgeier is Program Manager for WebFWD, helping Pascal to build the program through partnerships, curriculum development and process improvements, supporting the teams, and build it out through promotions, marketing, events and general shenanigans.

Prior to this role, she worked with various startups in marketing and communications roles and served 8 years in Charles Schwab's technology division as a product manager and Communications Director for the Chief Information Officer. Pre-Schwab she was an executive recruiter and venture capital associate in Silicon Valley and took time out to get her MBA at UC Berkeley's Haas School of Business.

When not schmoozing at an event or with Mozillians, Diane can be found putting her cycling students through torturous workouts.

Havi Hoffman

Havi Hoffman is a long-time web writer, editor, curator and content wrangler, herder of cats, and unabashed WebFWD fangirl. She sits in Mozilla Labs and helps get the word out about Mozilla’s experiments and projects beyond the browser.

Havi was at Yahoo! during the golden age of web-surfing, and for several years she directed Yahoo!’s global Open Hack program and headed Yahoo Press, a publishing partnership with O’Reilly Media, responsible for titles like Javascript: The Good Parts and Programming Social Applications.

She is thrilled to have found a place among the people of the red panda in the land of the open Web.
